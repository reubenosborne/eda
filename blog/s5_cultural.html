Core Skills

Learnings

I went into the 'Core' learning with confidence. Skimming over the content never produced something that I had not encountered before, or had not considered. I also knew about the material, considering it one of the aspects of Dev Academy that led me to study here.

I've reflected on this confidence as it could be a part of my ego that prevents me from pursuing greater depth in these skills or abilities. Thinking "Oh, I can do that" is a fixed mindset on something that has no end.

Challenges

Though the challenges were few, diging into my values and my identity had it's difficulties. I realised that it had been some time since I had stopped to consider my values and that I wasn't easily drawing to mind what they may be. Knowing what you value seems like a neccessary compass to guide you through life though I'm sure they guide whether you know them or not, being able to remind oneself of what you value seems like a useful tool to keep on track.

Exploring my values was far more valuable than I had expected, especially as I looked into my past to see where these may have been formed. Questioning what my values are, and why, allows me to strenghten my objectives in life and let go of things that are not serving me.

Why Core?

The focus on core skills within this curriculum makes perfect sense. Though developing can be a very isolated task, and primary relationships often form between human and computer, there is a lot of need for interpersonal skills and self awareness. Writing code is only one aspect of working in this industry and knowing how to be an effective part of a team not only saves time and money, it can lend itself to better products overall.

A well integrated team has developers being able to shape products with their insight. Being able to effectively communicate what you are observing in your work but also conveying this to other people in the team can improve projects.

I see this industry pushing back on one's mental and physical health with it's stresses (broken code, deadlines, potential for loss). Being aware of your headspace and taking the neccessary precautions for your health is essential. I've already witnessed how the body and mind feels after a day of getting lost in code without taking breaks.

Is it worth it?

Without a doubt. I see immense value to building a toolkit that is more than just technical ability. Self awareness and the ability to keep focused and clear benefits everyone.

A team needs to know how to look after theirselves as well as the team as a whole to thrive. Core skills enable this.
